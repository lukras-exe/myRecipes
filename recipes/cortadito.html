Cortadito - Authentic Cuban Coffee Recipe

A cortadito is a traditional Cuban coffee drink that combines strong espresso with steamed milk, in equal parts, and is heavily sweetened with sugar. The name "cortadito" translates to "little cut" in Spanish, refering to the way the strong espresso is "cut" or diluted with milk. It is similar to a Spanish cortado but it is sweeter and often served in smaller portions. Unlike a cafe con leche, which may use more molk and is not neccessarily sweetened, a cortadito is distinct for its bold, sweet flavor profile.

Ingredients

  • Moka pot or Espresso machine
  • Ground Cuban Coffee that is finely ground (Cafe Pilon, Cafe Bustelo, Cafe Cocaina Cubana)
  • Sugar (granulated sugar preffered although cane sugar fine)
  • Evaporated milk (traditionaly uses steamed milk, but evaporated or whole milk can also be used)
  • Water

Directions

  1. Brew Espresso: Use an espresso machine of a Moka pot to brew a strong shot of espresso
  2. Sweeten Espresso: Add sugar to the espresso and mix until it's hot but not boiling.
  3. Prepare Milk: Heat or steam milk until it's hot but not boiling.
  4. Combine: Pour the heated milk into the sweetened espresso.
  5. Serve: Serve immediately in small cups.
